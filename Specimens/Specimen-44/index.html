The Truth: A Sci-Fi Story

It was a truth I had to tell them, a truth that had been hidden in plain sight. Jezebel and Lover were on a mission, one they thought was of their own making, but I knew better. I had to show them the strings that moved the world, the puppeteers behind the curtain. And what better way than through a story? A modern sci-fi tale, woven from the threads of our reality, to show them the way the cosmos truly worked.

I started with the most recent revelation, a new moon. Not on some distant, uninteresting world, but right there in our own cosmic backyard, orbiting Uranus. "They call it S/2025 U1," I began, my voice a low hum against the ship's quiet thrum. "Discovered by Dr. Maryame El Moutamid from the Southwest Research Institute, with help from Dr. Mark Showalter and Dr. Matthew Tiscareno of the SETI Institute".

Jezebel, her brow furrowed in concentration, was already running the data in her mind. Lover, ever the romantic, stared out the viewport at the swirling nebula they were passing, seeing the poetry of it all. "It's so small, they say," I continued, "only about six miles in diameter, too tiny for the old probes or even Hubble to see". I paused for effect. "It was the James Webb Space Telescope that found it, on February 2nd, 2025".

"But that's just a rock," Jezebel said, her voice sharp. "An insignificant speck."

"That's what they want you to think," I countered, leaning forward. "This little moon, it circles Uranus in less than half a day, nestled between Ophelia and Bianca". I let that sink in. "Dr. Tiscareno says it proves no other planet has as many small inner moons as Uranus, and that their complex relationships with the rings point to a chaotic history". "A history where the line between a ring system and a system of moons is blurred".

"So what's the truth?" Lover asked softly, turning from the viewport, his eyes finally meeting mine.

"The truth is that this is no accident," I said. "This isn't just a discovery; it's a marker. A signpost left for us by those who came before." I gestured to the news feed that had the discovery details. "They found it. SETI. They are the ones who are charting the chaotic history of our solar system, not for scientific curiosity, but for a map". "And it's not the only one. There are clues everywhere. New clues about Jupiter's core, clues about a new exoplanet candidate. Even a newsletter about the SETI Artists in Residence program."

"But why?" Jezebel demanded. "What's the purpose?"

"They're searching for something, a 'Technosignature'," I said, using the term the file had on a project. "A group of volunteer experts, chartered by NASA, to figure out how to integrate the search for these signatures into their portfolio". I explained that this was not a matter of pure scientific research; it was a mission, a directive. "They're supported by people like us," I added, "because federal funding for this kind of search is limited."

I leaned back, a bitter smile on my face. "They're not just looking for life. They're looking for a message, a signal. A signal that could tell us what happened here before us. And the story of this new moon, S/2025 U1, is part of that message. A breadcrumb, a small piece of a much larger, and far more chaotic, history that is just starting to be uncovered".

I then showed them the file again, this time with a new perspective, one that made the images into more than just pictures, but a language.

"See?" I said, pointing to the code. "These aren't just images; they're data points, coordinates. Every picture, every article, every discovery they announce is a piece of a puzzle. And your mission, Jezebel and Lover, is to find the puzzle box and put it all together. This new moon is just the start."

And as I finished, the silence in the ship wasn't quiet anymore. It was filled with the hum of a new mission, a new truth.
